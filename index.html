Steve Jobs

"I want to put a ding in the universe."

Steve Jobs was the chairman, CEO, and co-founder of Apple. Here is a timeline of his life,

February 24, 1955: Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California and is adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
1970: Meets future apple co-founder, Steve Wozniak.
1972: Graduates high school, and goes to college. He drops out after a semester.
1976: Co-founds Apple Computer with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. That same year, the company sells the Apple I.
June 5, 1977:
June 5, 1977: Releases the Apple II, the first commercially available personal computer, and Apple's first successful personal computer.
September 12, 1985: Jobs resigns as Apple chairman
February 3, 1986: Buys Pixar studios
November 29, 1995: Becomes Pixar's president and CEO. Later in the year, the release of "Toy Story,"
December 10, 1996: Returns to apple.
July 9, 1997: Becomes Apple CEO.
May 6, 1998: Introduces the iMac.
January 9, 2001: Introduces iTunes,
October 23, 2001: Introduces the iPod
February 2, 2006: iTunes sells its billionth song.
January 27, 2010: Introduces the iPad. Apple sells 500,000 iPads during their first week on the market.
August 24, 2011: Resigns from Apple CEO post; becomes chairman.
October 5, 2011: Jobs passes away in Palo Alto, California.
